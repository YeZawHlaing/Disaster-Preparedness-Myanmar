Chemical Emergency Survival Guide

Before

  • Donâ€™t mix chemicals, even common household products. Some combinations, such as ammonia and bleach, can create toxic gases.
  • Store chemical products properly.
  • Keep non-food products tightly closed in their original containers for easy identification.
  • Dispose of unused chemicals properly to avoid contamination.
  • Do not smoke or light fire in identified hazardous areas.
  • Avoid staying near industries that process hazardous chemicals, if possible.
  • Keep emergency contact numbers handy, including those of nearby hazardous industries.
  • Participate in capacity-building programs organized by the government, voluntary organizations, or industrial units.
  • Identify safe shelters along with safe and easy access routes.
  • Prepare a family disaster management plan.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essential items for safety and survival.

During

  • Do not panic. Evacuate quickly through the designated escape route.
  • Keep a wet piece of cloth on your face while evacuating.
  • If you are unable to evacuate, close all doors and windows tightly.
  • Once you reach a safe location, inform emergency services (police, hospital, etc.).
  • Do not spread or believe in rumors.

After

  • Do not consume uncovered food or water.
  • Change into fresh clothes after reaching a safe place/shelter, and wash hands properly.

Community

  • Make the entire neighborhood aware of chemical hazards and the necessary first aid.
  • Listen to the radio, watch TV, and check the Internet for official news and announcements.
  • Provide accurate information to government officials.
  • Sensitize authorities about the exact requirements for protective equipment needed to handle the hazard.
